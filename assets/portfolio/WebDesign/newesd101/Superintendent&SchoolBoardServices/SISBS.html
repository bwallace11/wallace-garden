Superintendent and Principal Search Services

NEWESD 101 is available to assist school districts with superintendent and principal searches. In collaboration with the district superintendent and school board, our team will provide comprehensive support throughout the hiring process.

Services Provided

  • Position brochure development
  • Custom application form creation
  • Advertising on the NEWESD website
  • Review of candidate applications
  • Interview schedule coordination
  • Participation in the interview process
  • Correspondence with candidates

New Superintendent Orientation

NEWESD will invite new superintendents to our offices or travel to your district to review the critical elements of district superintendency. Our comprehensive orientation covers essential topics including:

  • Overview of regional meetings and associations (NEWASA, sub-regional superintendent meetings)
  • State meetings and conferences relevant to your role
  • General items of importance for new superintendents
  • Required forms and documentation
  • District compliance requirements

Strategic Planning Facilitation

NEWESD provides expert facilitation for district strategic planning processes. Our structured approach typically takes approximately 6 months and ensures meaningful community engagement and actionable outcomes.

Strategic Planning Process

  1. Committee Formation: Assemble a planning committee including the superintendent, board members, representative staff, and community members. All meetings with three or more board members must be advertised as Board Strategic Plan work sessions.
  2. Mission Review: Begin with your district's mission statement and bring meaning and direction to that mission through strategic planning.
  3. Identify Core Tenets: In the first meeting, discuss what the mission means and reach agreement on 2-4 basic tenets to include in your strategic plan. No more than four tenets should be selected to maintain focus.
  4. Develop Each Tenet: One tenet per meeting, describe how it will be implemented and identify supporting action items. This phase typically requires 3-4 meetings depending on the number of tenets.
  5. Draft Review: Create a draft plan to share with staff and community for input, questions, and feedback.
  6. Finalization: Hold a final planning group meeting to review feedback, followed by board adoption at a subsequent meeting.

Note: This planning process requires commitment from all participants. It is most productive when members attend all meetings rather than coming and going throughout the process.
